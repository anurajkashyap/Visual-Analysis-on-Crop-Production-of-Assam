Introduction

Agriculture is one of the most important primary occupation in Assam. Around 80% of the population of Assam is directly or indirectly dependent on agriculture in some way or another. so, the economy, development and many more important aspects of the people of Assam is somewhat related to agricultural factors of Assam. Crop production in Assam is depends on a large number of factors ranging from season type to Area of cultivation.

We are going to conduct a visual analysis on the Crop production in Assam, and try to convey some basic but important conclusions that may help us to understand the agricultural environment of Assam.

Dataset Description

We are going to use the Crop Production in India Dataset for this project. Anyone willing to look into the dataset can access it through the link https://www.kaggle.com/datasets/abhinand05/crop-production-in-india?select=crop_production.csv.

The dataset consist of data for the whole India, but we are using a subset of the data containing only information about the crop production of Assam for our analysis.

Summary of Analysis

We have done a visual analysis on the Crop production in Assam between 1997 & 2014 and we have found some interesting conclusion regarding this. Some of them are:

  1. The correlation between Area and Production of a particular crop is mostly between 8 and 1, showing a strong positive correlation between Area and Production for a particular crop.

  2. Coconut is the most produced crop during the period between 1997 and 2014, although the production after 2010 has diminised a lot.

  3. Rice is the seondmost produced crop during the time period. It is one of the most consistent crop whose production kept on increasing steadily over the period of time, but its production for the autumn season is dcreasing over time.

  4. Among the 10 most produced crop during the time period, only 6 of the 10 crops are produced during the whole year, other 4 were seasonal crops.

  5. For Rice, we found that Nagaon is the district with the highest production and its production is roughly around \(\frac{1}{8}th\) of the total production of the state.

  6. Nagaon is also the leading producer of Paddy, Sugarcane, Banana & Jute in Assam which are also among the most produced crops in Assam making Nagaon one of the most agricultural friendly district in Assam.
